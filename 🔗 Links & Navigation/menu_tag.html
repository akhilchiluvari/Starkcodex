<menu> - List/Menu Container

🧠 What it does:

  • Represents a list or menu of commands, options, or links.
  • Originally intended for context menus or toolbars.
  • In modern HTML5, it's often just treated like a semantic wrapper for lists of navigation items.

🕰️ When to use:

  • To group together navigation or interactive commands.
  • If you want a semantic alternative to <ul> for menus.
  • Rarely used today, but can appear in semantic HTML structures.

💻 Syntax:

<menu>
    <li><a href="home.html">Home</a></li>
    <li><a href="about.html">About</a></li>
    <li><a href="contact.html">Contact</a></li>
</menu>

🔍 Output:

  • Home
  • About
  • Contact
